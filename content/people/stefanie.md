Professor Stefanie Gutschmidt leads research at the intersection of sound and motion, spanning topics from nanoscale multiphysics interactions to macroscale ecological acoustics. Her expertise encompasses nonlinear dynamics and vibrations, MEMS sensor technology, collective oscillatory systems, fluid–structure interactions, and acoustics. Her research is enabled by collaborative, cross-disciplinary approaches that bring together engineers, physicists, biologists, ecologists, mathematicians, and computer scientists. At the University of Canterbury, she co-directs both the Listening Lab and the Dynamics Systems Lab.

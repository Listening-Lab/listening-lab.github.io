### Field data of Common Brushtail Possum (*Trichosurus vulpecula*) vocalisations

This dataset contains field recordings of the Common Brushtail Possum, an ecologically destructive invasive mammalian predator in New Zealand. Designed to aid automated pest monitoring networks, it represents a benchmark dataset for detecting sparse nocturnal animal vocalisations amidst heavy environmental confounding noise.

[**Download the Dataset on Kaggle →**](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/0f51f43c1b9340d2180656990e32532a3e87afc8520f153111b6ba39ebcad073)

### Dataset Structure

The core dataset contains 5-second audio segments (`.WAV`) downsampled to **16 kHz**, alongside the raw uncompressed field recordings:

* **Training Set:** 3,000 segments balanced between verified possum vocalisations (1,062) and hard false-positives (1,938).
* **Validation Set:** 500 segments evenly split between possum vocalisations (255) and environmental noise (245).
* **Raw Field Audio:** 1,236 continuous 5-minute parent recordings sampled at **48 kHz**.

#### Ground Truth & Filenames

Filenames follow the strict temporal mapping `YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS_RX.WAV`:
* `20220607_003000` defines the capture timestamp (e.g., 3:00 AM on 7 June 2022).
* `R0` denotes the specific segment index sliced from the 5-minute parent audio file.
* Ground truth binary classes: `possum` or `false-positive`.

*Note: The `false-positive` class aggregates diverse real-world ecological confounders including rain, wind gusts, highway traffic, sheep 🐑, and native avifauna.*

#### Interested in expanding this dataset?

Current annotations support binary `{possum, noise}` tasks. Work is required to extend annotation specificity to sub-classes (e.g. categorising distinct possum call types or specific noise sources). Get in touch if your group is interested in collaborating on multi-class labels.

*Dataset labels were generated using our open-source [Listening Lab Annotator](https://github.com/Listening-Lab/Annotator).*
